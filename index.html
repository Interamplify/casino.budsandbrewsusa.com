Most popular category at every casino on this list. Modern video slots pack bonus rounds, free spins, cascading reels, multipliers, and Megaways mechanics into increasingly creative packages. But the number that separates good slots from bad ones is RTP — Return to Player.

A slot with 96% RTP returns C$96 per C$100 wagered over millions of spins. Blood Suckers pays back 98%. Mega Joker hits 99%. Starmania sits at 97.87%. These aren't guarantees for any individual session, but over hundreds of hours, that 2–3% difference between a 94% RTP slot and a 97% one compounds into real money. You can check any slot's RTP by clicking the info icon inside the game. If a slot hides its RTP, skip it.

Gates of Olympus, Rise of Olympus, Big Bass Amazon, and Sugar Rush 1000 are popular across our top 10. TonyBet carries 9,300+ titles. Casino Days stocks 6,000+ from 40+ studios.

Blackjack

Canada's favourite online table game, and the one with the best odds if you learn basic strategy. The house edge drops below 0.5% — meaning for every C$100 you bet, the expected cost is under 50 cents. No other table game comes close.

Look for European variations with few decks and a 3:2 Blackjack payout. Avoid insurance bets and side bets — they carry much worse odds than the base game. Strategy charts are free and available everywhere online. Print one out, keep it next to your screen, and use it until the decisions become automatic. Jackpot City and 888casino have the strongest blackjack libraries.

Roulette

Always play European or French. Single-zero European roulette carries a 2.7% house edge. American roulette adds a double zero and jumps to 5.26%. Over 100 spins at C$10 each, that difference costs you roughly C$25 more on the American wheel. French roulette with the La Partage rule is even better — it returns half your even-money bet when the ball lands on zero, cutting the effective edge to 1.35%.

Legal Guide

Gambling Laws Across Canada

No single law. Ten provinces, three territories, and a patchwork of rules.

Canada's Criminal Code doesn't explicitly permit online gambling. What it does is let each province regulate gambling inside its borders. That creates a different legal landscape depending on where you live.

No law prohibits individual Canadians from using international online casinos either. But every casino on this page holds a Canadian-regulated iGaming Ontario licence, so the offshore grey area doesn't apply here.

Ontario

Launched its regulated open-market iGaming system in April 2022 through the AGCO and iGaming Ontario. Minimum age: 19 for casinos, 18 for lotteries. Over 70 licensed operators compete in the province — including every casino on this page. Ontario players have the widest legal selection in Canada.

British Columbia

BCLC oversees all gambling, regulated by the Gaming Policy and Enforcement Branch. PlayNow.com is the only regulated online platform. Minimum age: 19.

Alberta

AGLC regulates gambling for residents aged 18+. PlayAlberta.ca is the sole regulated online platform. The province plans to open its market to private operators later this year.

Quebec

Loto-Quebec and the RACJ regulate gambling. Minimum age: 18. Espacejeux is the only legal online platform. Quebec actively blocks unlicensed offshore sites.

Manitoba

Regulated by MBLL and the LGCA. PlayNow.com operates as the online platform. Minimum age: 18.

Saskatchewan

Saskatchewan Liquor and Gaming Authority regulates under the Alcohol and Gaming Regulation Act. PlayNow.com has operated since November 2022 through a SIGA-BCLC partnership. Minimum age: 19.

Atlantic Provinces

The Atlantic Lottery Corporation oversees all four provinces — Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, PEI, and Newfoundland. Minimum age: 19 across the board. Online gambling restricted to ALC platforms. Newfoundland has no casinos.

The Territories

Western Canada Lottery Corporation covers NWT, Nunavut, and Yukon. Minimum age: 19. Yukon has one seasonal casino. The other two territories have none. Online options are extremely limited.

Taxes on Winnings

The Canada Revenue Agency doesn't tax recreational gambling winnings. Your casino payouts are exempt from income tax and capital gains tax. The one exception: if the CRA decides gambling is a business activity for you — meaning it's a primary or regular income source. Professional gamblers face different rules. For anything beyond casual play, talk to a tax advisor.
